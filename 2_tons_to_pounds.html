First of all, you should know a little about the history of tons and pounds

A ton is a measure of mass and weight. The ton, commonly referred to as the short ton, is 2,000 pounds, or 908 kilograms, in the United States and Canada. The ton, sometimes known as the long ton, is equal to 2,240 avoirdupois pounds, or 1,016 kilograms, in the United Kingdom. The tonne, sometimes referred to as the metric ton in the International System of Units (SI), is defined as 2,204.623 pounds, or 1,000 kilograms. The long ton is used in the United Kingdom along with other countries that still use the imperial system, the short ton is used mainly in the United States, and the tonne is used worldwide

A pound (symbol: lb) is a mass unit that is utilized by the imperial and US customary measuring systems. The precise weight of the international avoirdupois pound, or today's standard pound, is 0.45359237 kilos. 16 avoirdupois ounces make up an avoirdupois pound. The pound as a unit of weight is widely used in the United States, often for measuring body weight. Many versions of the pound existed in the past in the United Kingdom (UK), and although the UK largely uses the International System of Units
